Cold vs Warm weather plants

Frost affects plants differently depending on whether they are adapted to cold or warm weather. Cold-weather plants, such as kale, and carrots, can tolerate frost because they have natural protections that prevent their cells from freezing, and frost can make them taste sweeter. In contrast, warm-weather plants such as tomatoes and basil are sensitive to frost. Freezing temperatures temperatures cause ice to form inside their cells, damaging or killing the plant. While hardy plants can survive and recover, tender plants often suffer severe damage even from a light frost.

Why Frost Dates?

Frost dates are important because they help gardeners know when it is safe to plant and protect their crops. The last frost date in spring signals when it's usually safe to plant warm-weather plants without risk of damage, while the first frost date in fall warns when cold temperatures may begin to harm or kill them. By understanding these dates, gardeners can plan planting times, extend growing seasons, and avoid losing plants to unexpected frost.
